The turtle spieces

There are five species of turtles that can be found in Sri Lanka. they have varying nesting and foraging habits along the country's coastline. It's important to note that the presence and population of these species can vary, and conservation efforts play a crucial role in their protection.

Green Turtle

  • Known as "Rathu Hora" in Sinhala.
  • They are the most commonly seen turtle species in Sri Lanka.
  • Popular nesting sites:
    1. Rekawa Beach
    2. Kosgoda Beach
    3. Pigeon Island

Loggerhead Turtle

  • Known as "Dumbara Hora" in Sinhala.
  • They are a less common but occasional visitor to Sri Lanka's shores.
  • They mainly nest in the eastern part of the island.
  • Popular nesting sites:
    1. Panama Beach
    2. Uppuveli Beach

Olive Ridley Turtle

  • Known as "Rathu Kambala Hora" in Sinhala.
  • They are relatively small-sized turtles and are globally endangered.
  • Olive Ridleys nest in large numbers along the eastern coast of Sri Lanka.
  • Popular nesting sites:
    1. Rekawa Beach
    2. Panama Beach
    3. Pottuvil Point

Hawksbill Turtle

  • Known as "Pachcha Kambala Hora" in Sinhala.
  • They are critically endangered and have a beautiful shell.
  • Hawksbill turtles nest along Sri Lanka's coasts but in smaller numbers.
  • Popular nesting sites:
    1. Rekawa Beach
    2. Pigeon Island
    3. Koggala Beach

Leatherback Turtle

  • The leatherback turtle (Dermochelys coriacea) is the largest species of sea turtle and is an endangered species.
  • In Sri Lanka, leatherback turtles can be found nesting on certain beaches during the nesting season.
