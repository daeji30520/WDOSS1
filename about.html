Nesting Site Protection

Turtle hatcheries actively monitor and protect nesting sites to ensure the safety of sea turtle eggs. They often mark and relocate nests to safeguard them from human disturbance or predation. This helps to improve the survival rate of the eggs.

Egg Collection & Incubation

Hatcheries collect sea turtle eggs from nesting beaches to prevent poaching and predation. The collected eggs are carefully transported to the hatchery where they are incubated under controlled conditions. This increases the chances of successful hatching and survival of the hatchlings.

Hatchling Care

Once the eggs hatch, hatcheries provide care for the newborn hatchlings. This involves ensuring suitable environmental conditions, including temperature and humidity, and providing a safe space for the hatchlings to develop and gain strength before their release.

Predation Prevention

To protect hatchlings from predators, hatcheries may use enclosures or netted areas. These structures allow the hatchlings to make their way to the ocean safely, reducing the risk of predation during this vulnerable stage.

Release Programs

Hatcheries organize release programs where visitors and volunteers can participate in releasing the hatchlings into the ocean. These programs not only create awareness but also give the hatchlings a better chance of survival by helping them navigate to the sea.

Rehabilitation & Rescue

Some turtle hatcheries also serve as rehabilitation centers for injured or sick turtles. They provide medical care, nursing, and rehabilitation support to injured turtles until they are healthy enough to be released back into the wild.
