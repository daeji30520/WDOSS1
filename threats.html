Pollution and Marine Debris

WHAT IS IS

Pollution from plastic debris, oil spills, and chemical contaminants can harm sea turtles through ingestion or entanglement.

HOW TO SOLVE IT

Conducting regular beach clean-ups, promoting recycling and proper waste management practices, and educating communities about the impacts of pollution on marine life can help reduce the threat of pollution to turtles.

Fishing Activities

WHAT IT IS

Sea turtles can become accidentally entangled in fishing nets or trapped in fishing gear, resulting in injury or death

HOW TO SOLVE IT

Promoting the use of turtle-friendly fishing practices such as the use of TEDs (Turtle Excluder Devices) in fishing nets, providing training to fishermen on proper handling and release techniques, and enforcing regulations to minimize turtle bycatch can mitigate this threat.

Habitat Loss and Degradation

WHAT IT IS

Coastal development, pollution, and beach erosion can destroy nesting habitats and feeding grounds for sea turtles.

HOW TO SOLVE IT

Implementing strict coastal zone management regulations, promoting sustainable tourism practices, and conducting regular beach clean-ups can help protect and restore turtle habitats.
